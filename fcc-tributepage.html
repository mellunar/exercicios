FCC Tribute Page - Marie Curie

Marie Curie

The first person and the only woman to earn Nobel Prize twice.

Marie Curie's early life timeline:

  • 1867 - Born in Warsaw, Poland, as Maria Skłodowska.
  • 1885 - At that time, women weren't allowed to enroll a regular institution of higher grade, so she and her sister, Bronisława, became involved with the clandestine Flying University, a institution that admitted women students.
  • 1890 - Her sister gets married and moves to Paris, France, inviting her, but she declines because couldn't afford an university tuition.
  • 1891 - She leaves Poland to study in University of Paris and changes her name to Marie.
  • 1894 - She mets Pierre Curie, gets her second degree in physics and returns to Poland for summer break, where she thought that could work in her chosen field, but was denied a place at Kraków University because of sexism in academia. A letter from Pierre convinced her to return to Paris to pursue a Ph.D.
  • 1895 - At her insistence, Pierre Curie had written up his research on magnetism and received his own doctorate in March. They marry in July.
  • 1896 - Influenced by two important discoveries involving uranium, she decided to look into the element's rays as a possible field of research for a thesis. She hypothesized that the radiation was not the outcome of some interaction of molecules but must come from the atom itself.
